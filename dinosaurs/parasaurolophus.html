Parasaurolophus

Dino Profile

  • Period: Late Cretaceous
  • Diet: Herbivore
  • Location: North America

About the Parasaurolophus

Parasaurolophus was a duck-billed dinosaur famous for its long, hollow, backward-curving crest. Scientists believe this crest was used to make loud, trumpet-like calls for communication, species recognition, or attracting mates. It walked on two legs or four, depending on speed, and lived in herds near rivers and forests, feeding on plants.
