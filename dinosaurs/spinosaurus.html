Spinosaurus

Dino Profile

  • Period: Late Cretaceous
  • Diet: Carnivore (Piscivore)
  • Location: North Africa

About the Spinosaurus

Spinosaurus is one of the most unique and debated dinosaurs. It is believed to be the largest of all carnivorous dinosaurs, even larger than T-Rex. It had a long, crocodile-like snout and cone-shaped teeth, perfect for catching fish. Recent discoveries suggest it was semi-aquatic, with a paddle-like tail for swimming.
